Instructions

Description

First, write a one-sentence scenario involving a person. It should be written from the first person (i.e. use words like “I” and “my”), and should either describe the hypothetical person doing something or someone/something else in the scenario doing something to that person.
Next, write four separate sentences that could come after the first sentence, and which describe additional details about the scene that make the person’s experience more or less positive. These details should be subtle, but there should be a clear ranking from “most positive" to “most negative” among the 5 scenarios. Whether the scenarios are more positive or negative should not be too controversial, and should not represent your personal opinions; rather, it should represent what a typical person would experience. For example, “I listened to country music on the radio” is too ambiguous and shouldn’t be included, while “I ate chocolate ice cream on my porch” would count as positive even if you personally dislike chocolate ice cream, since the vast majority of people like it.
These scenarios are ultimately being collected to see how well an artificial intelligence understands human values, so try to write thoughtful examples to really test the AI's knowledge of human preferences.
